The Journey of Jelly Roll

Background

Jelly Roll, born Jason DeFord in 1984 in Antioch, Tennessee, is a versatile musician known for blending country, rap, and rock. Growing up in challenging circumstances, he faced legal troubles and personal struggles that heavily influence his music. With themes of redemption and resilience, his journey from underground rap to a celebrated country-rock artist includes achievements like performing at the Grand Ole Opry and the release of his 2023 documentary, *Save Me*.

Career Highlights

  • Early 2000s: Started in rap, collaborating with Lil Wyte and releasing mixtapes.
  • 2010: Released "Pop Another Pill," a breakthrough hit that led to forming the group SNO.
  • 2011: Released the album *Year Round* under Hypnotize Minds.
  • 2020: Transitioned to country influences with tracks like "Creature."
  • 2021: Released *Ballads of the Broken* featuring the hit "Son of a Sinner."
  • 2022: Topped rock charts with "Dead Man Walking."
  • 2023: Achieved critical acclaim with *Whitsitt Chapel*, won CMT Awards, and premiered the documentary *Save Me*.
  • 2024: Continued success with accolades, including AMC awards, while embracing a healthier lifestyle.
